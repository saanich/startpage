What computer & monitor will I receive?

You will receive the computer and monitor identified during the needs assessment. We now have six types of computers and two monitor types. Also, some meeting rooms will have computers and projectors installed.

In order to provide better service and a better computing experience, IT is setting new standards for the computers that will be supported across the organization. These standards are based on the specifications needed for different types of computing use.

After lengthy assessment, IT selected the following brand/models as our new standard supported computers:

How are computers assigned?

Over the past three months, IT has completed a needs assessment to determine the type of computer everyone receives. This included completion of a needs assessment questionnaire and discussions with your managers.

The majority of staff will be given either a Standard Desktop or a Virtual PC and experience very good performance. However, there are two considerations that may change the computer type you receive:

The software you use: some software such as Adobe InDesign or AutoCAD requires more powerful computers (Power or High Power Desktops)

Your need to have remote access: if you need to have a computer away from your desk or meeting room, a laptop is assigned (Standard or Power Laptop). [Note: we will have computers and projectors in Committee Rooms 1, 2 (Hall), 3, 4 (Annex) and Public Works (Yard)

What is a Virtual PC?

What is a Virtual PC?

Virtual PCs are an alternative to the Standard Desktop and will be a growing part of Saanich’s computing strategy.

Virtual PCs will be delivered to a portion of staff as the first part of a transition to this new platform over several years.
